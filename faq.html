Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about shark tracking and our platform.

How accurate is the shark tracking data?

Our tracking data is highly accurate, with most tags providing location data within 100-200 meters. The accuracy depends on several factors including satellite coverage, weather conditions, and whether the shark is at the surface (where GPS signals are strongest). We use advanced algorithms to filter and refine the raw data to provide the most precise locations possible.

How often is the tracking data updated?

Update frequency varies depending on the tag type and the shark's behavior. Most of our satellite tags transmit data every time the shark surfaces, which can range from multiple times per day to once every few days. Our system processes and displays new data as soon as it's received from the satellites.

Does tagging harm the sharks?

All our tagging procedures follow strict ethical guidelines and are conducted by trained professionals. The tags are designed to minimize impact, and most are programmed to detach after a set period (typically 6-12 months). Research shows that properly attached tags don't affect sharks' behavior, feeding, or migration patterns in the long term.

Can I adopt or sponsor a shark?

Yes! Our Shark Sponsorship program allows individuals and organizations to support the tracking and conservation of specific sharks. Sponsors receive regular updates about their shark's movements, photos when available, and a certificate of sponsorship. Funds go directly toward tag maintenance, data analysis, and conservation efforts.

How can I contribute to shark conservation?

There are many ways to help: sponsor a shark through our program, donate to conservation organizations, reduce your seafood consumption or choose sustainable options, participate in beach cleanups, educate others about sharks, and support policies that protect marine ecosystems. Every action counts toward preserving these vital ocean predators.

Why do some sharks disappear from tracking?

There are several reasons a shark might stop transmitting: the tag's battery may have died, the tag detached as programmed, the shark may be in an area with poor satellite coverage, or in rare cases, the shark may have died. We make every effort to recover tags when possible to download any remaining data.
